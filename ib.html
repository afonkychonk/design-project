  • The IB

What is the IB program?
  • The IB programs encourage both personal development and academic achievement challenging students to think critically, to ask the right questions and think across disciplines. An IB education also fosters diversity, curiosity and a healthy appetite for learning.
How is the IB different to the GCE?
  • The IBDP program is continues, as found in previous curriculum, a broad and balanced approach to subject choices. The GCE A levels tend to be more specialized and focused on a few subject areas.
Is the IB right for me?
  • The IB system is suited for a well-rounded and international education for an interconnected future.
  • It places emphasis on self-directed learning, social and emotional development, and independency.
  • It encourages a global perspective through language, humanities, and international interaction.
  • Useful for expat students as they can transition to many countries.
What is the goal of the IB?
  • The IB philosophy is to create a worldwide community of schools, educators and students with a shared vision and mission to empower young people with the skills, values and knowledge to create a better and more peaceful world.
How is the IB graded?
  • The maximum score in the MYP is 56 with a minimum requirement of 3 or higher in each eAssessment to receive the MYP Certificate. Scores a calculated from 1-7 for each class, 7 being the highest grade.
  • Similarly in the DP, scores are given from 1-7 and are given a grade for each DP course attempted. The diploma is given to students who gain at least 24 points and fulfill the 3 elements of the DP. This includes the theory of knowledge (TOK) and extended essay (EE) which can contribute an additional 3 points to the overall score. Creativity, activity, service (CAS), the final element, does not contribute to points but is required.
Do universities recognize the IB?
  • Universities around the globe recognize the IB, students send exam results to these institutions in more than 90 countries annually. Many universities value the holistic and rigorous education the IB provides.
